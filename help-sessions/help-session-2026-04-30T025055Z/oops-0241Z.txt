Object body: full 2048 bytes of 0x6b (SLUB_RED_INACTIVE poison) — i.e. the
object has been freed and poisoned cleanly. Last byte of object: 0xa5
(SLUB_RED_ACTIVE end marker, immediately preceding the redzone).

Right redzone (8 bytes — should all be 0xbb):
  ffff8a5a617b7000: 72 1b 90 27 4f a3 19 04   r..'O...

Padding past redzone is intact (0x5a everywhere) — so this was a single
8-byte overwrite into the 8-byte right redzone, not a long buffer overrun.

Decoded little-endian as u64: 0x0419a34f27901b72 — does NOT look like a
kernel pointer (kernel pointers on x86_64 are 0xffff8x.... or 0xffffffff8x....)
and does NOT look like a userspace pointer pattern. Looks closer to junk /
DMA-streamed bytes than a stale stack/heap pointer.
